Most small business owners handle HR on their own — until they can't. Hiring your first employee, navigating a difficult termination, or trying to stay compliant with changing regulations can quickly reveal the limits of a DIY approach.

HR Solutions gives you access to experienced human resources professionals without the cost of a full-time hire. But how do you know when you need it?

## You're Hiring Your First Employee

Bringing on your first employee is exciting — and complicated. You need employment agreements, an onboarding process, a W-4, an I-9, and a clear understanding of your obligations as an employer. Getting this foundation right from day one prevents costly problems later.

## Your Handbook Is Outdated (Or You Don't Have One)

An employee handbook isn't just a formality. It sets expectations, protects your business, and ensures consistent treatment of all employees. If yours is out of date — or you've never had one — you're exposed.

## You're Facing an Employee Relations Issue

Performance problems, interpersonal conflicts, complaints, or potential terminations all carry legal and operational risk. Having an HR professional in your corner helps you navigate these situations the right way.

## You're Struggling to Stay Compliant

Employment law changes constantly. Minimum wage updates, leave requirements, classification rules, ADA accommodations — keeping up is a job in itself. HR Solutions professionals stay current so you don't have to.

## You're Growing Quickly

When your headcount is growing, your HR needs grow with it. Benefits administration, onboarding systems, performance management — fractional HR scales with you so you always have the right level of support.

## You've Had a Close Call

If you've ever said "I should probably have an HR person look at this," that instinct is worth listening to. Whether it's a complaint, a termination, or a personnel policy question, having professional HR support before something becomes a problem is always the better path.
